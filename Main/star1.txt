Alpha Centauri is the closest star system to 
the Solar System at a distance of 4.37 lightyears.
It consists of three stars: the pair Alpha 
Centauri A and Alpha Centauri B and faint 
red dwarf, Proxima Centauri. To the naked 
eye, the two main components appear as a 
single object of an apparent visual 
magnitude of -0.27, forming the brightest star 
in the constellation Centaurus and the 
third-brightest star in the night sky.
